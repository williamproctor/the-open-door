The Vision

What If Scripture Could Be Seen
the Way It Was Meant to Be Felt?

Revelation chapter 4 is one of the most visually dense passages in the Bible — a throne of crystalline turquoise, four living creatures covered in eyes, 24 elders casting golden crowns across a sea of glass. Generations of artists have tried to capture it. Most have fallen short — reducing the sublime to the illustrative, the overwhelming to the merely ornamental.


The Open Door is a proof of concept — an entirely AI-generated cinematic short film that treats these sacred images with the reverence, scale, and visual sophistication they demand. Not a slideshow. Not an animation. A film.

The Narrative

Three Acts. One Threshold.

I

The Exile

Patmos. A stone dwelling at dusk. John — aged, exiled, alone — writes by lamplight. The palette is muted, earthy, terrestrial. Everything is heavy. Everything is before.

II

The Threshold

A door of blazing light materializes in his room. A voice like a trumpet. The world stretches. John is pulled through a dimensional membrane into heaven’s throne room — vast, crystalline, overwhelmingly luminous.

III

The Revelation

The throne. The creatures. The elders falling. Crowns cast across a sea of glass. An architecture of worship laid bare — then the slow dissolve back to a man, sitting at a table, forever changed.

Design Philosophy

Radiant Threshold

A proprietary visual language built on the premise that the most sacred moments in human experience occur at the boundary between what can be seen and what can only be felt.


Light is not illumination — it is material. Something that can be layered, sculpted, and accumulated with the same deliberateness a mason lays stone. Every composition must feel as though someone spent countless hours calibrating the precise point where gold becomes white, where emerald dissolves into atmosphere, where form surrenders to radiance.

Cinematography

Shot on a Virtual Camera

Every frame specifies a real-world lens equivalent, depth of field, and camera movement. This isn’t a storyboard — it’s a director’s shot list, designed for execution.

14mm Ultra-Wide
Overwhelming scale — the throne room’s vastness, the aerial view, the crystalline sea
85mm Portrait
Intimacy — John’s face, the man-faced creature, the elder’s fall
200mm Telephoto
Compression & power — isolating the throne, creature reveals at f/1.2
100mm Macro
Detail & texture — the golden crown on glass, materiality made sacred
ASPECT RATIO
2.39:1
Anamorphic Widescreen
DEPTH OF FIELD AS STORYTELLING
Deep when the space itself is the subject.
Shallow when emotion is the subject.
Ultra-shallow for maximum intimacy.
Rack focus for transitions between worlds.
COLOR TEMPERATURE ARC
Cool earth tones → the break → full heavenly warmth → return to earth, but warmer than before.

The Medium

Why Generative AI?

The most sacred moments in human experience occur at the boundary between what can be seen and what can only be felt.
ACCESSIBILITY

Traditional cinematic depictions of Revelation require massive studio budgets. GenAI democratizes visual storytelling — a single creator with deep domain knowledge can produce gallery-quality visual narrative.

SCRIPTURAL FIDELITY

Scripture describes phenomena that defy physical photography — faces consumed by light, creatures covered in eyes, seas of crystal. GenAI can render the impossible as the plausible.

ITERATION AT SCALE

The character sheets and prompts form a reproducible pipeline. Each entity can be regenerated at higher fidelity as models improve — the creative direction persists, the execution sharpens.
